Assignment 3 Group work

Date posted: Tuesday 1st September 2020

When making the pitch to my group I found that it was important to try and explain my idea in a clear simple way to best express my concept in a way that everyone could understand. Scope was one of the main points that my group discussed when considering each other's pitches. While everyone had a good pitch, some ideas were not feasible for our skill level or time restraint. Collectively we chose the pitch that we thought best suited the restraints of the project and was an idea that we were all interested in. Once we had chosen our project idea, we had to consider the direction we wanted the project to go in order to narrow it down to a manageable concept. We soon realised that a website for small businesses could have a very large scope and audience base which would be unrealistic for us to try and tackle. Therefore, we narrowed it down to hospitality businesses that meet our criteria of local, sustainable/environmentally friendly, cost efficient, and charitable.

Assignment 3 User flow

Date posted: Thursday 3rd September 2020

My group started by examining our user stories and planning out all of the pages that our website will need to be able to meet our users' needs. So far, we have identified the need for 5 pages: Home page, nomination page, filtering/search page, directory and profile/business page. Planning out how people will navigate and interact with our site is an important step that requires a lot of attention to detail. The user flow diagram will allow us to visually evaluate how well different users can navigate the site and achieve their user tasks and needs.

User Testing

Date posted: Thursday 22nd September 2020

As a group we came up with some tasks for our users to complete when testing our prototype. We wanted to test out our main navigation points and our filtering and search functions. These are key aspects of our website that we need to get right to create a website that user can actually interact with. We hope to receive feedback and insights that will aid us in improving our website and ensuring users enjoy the experience. We also wrote a few questions to ask the users during testing to gain more feedback on the design and experience of our prototype to ensure the content is clearly communicated and relevant. User testing will be a valuable step in our design process as it will allow us to see how a user interacts with our site and identify what parts of the interface confuse people and work to fix them.

Prototype

Date posted: Thursday 24th September 2020

We presented our prototype to the class today and received a lot of helpful feedback that will allow us to refine our prototype. Along with this feedback, we also gathered the information we got from our user testing. This feedback and testing results enables us to pinpoint the elements of our design that do not work or need to be improved to ensure we enhance the user experience. Some of the main aspects that were identified are the layout of the home page, text spacing, a lack of contrast on the buttons compared to other elements, and our design of the nomination and editors pages as these could use some more refinement to help finalise them.

Project progression

Date posted: Tuesday 29th September 2020

Today my group worked on incorporating the feedback that we received from the presentations into our prototype. We have expanded out our editors page to provide more information about each editor and make it a more important page as it is a main part of our website. We also changed our navigation layout as we found that the current buttons we were using did not respond well to a smaller page size. We now have a horizontal nav at the top of the page with buttons on either corner to navigate to the editors page and nominations page. The feedback we have received has allowed us to continue to improve and enhance our prototypes interactivity and overall design.

Prototype refinement

Date posted: Thursday 1st October 2020

Over the past few days we have implemented more of the feedback we received on Tuesday and created the hover states for our buttons, refined our home page and icons and reworked the colour scheme to ensure readability. Our original orange colour did not meet the contrast ratio test as the white text on orange background was not very readable. To fix this we decided to bring in a navy blue and use the orange as an accent colour. While this new colour scheme differs from our original, it is important that the text is always readable. With the final refinements of our prototype coming together, I created a GitHub repository for our group to start working on coding our website. GitHub allows us to work collaboratively and produce our website quicker as our group can work on the code separately on branches and push it up to the same repository.
